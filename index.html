Incinerated

Burning plastic releases CO₂ and other greenhouse gases.

Plastic production already accounts for 3.4% of global emissions. Burning waste adds even more to that footprint.

And even the most advanced systems can’t eliminate toxic byproducts entirely.

Where it sits for 500+ years...

Plastic doesn't biodegrade — it just quietly accumulates. Over time, it breaks into microplastics that drift into soil, groundwater and air.

Half of all plastic ever made is now sitting in these slow-motion leak points.

are mismanaged

This includes:

  • Uncontrolled dumpsites: Open heaps without lining or containment, common in low-income countries but present worldwide.
  • Open burning: Waste set on fire in backyards or roadside piles, producing toxic emissions—dioxins, furans, heavy metals—with no filtration.
  • Leaked to environment: Litter from streets, plastic spilling from landfills, debris escaping storm drains.

Much of it escapes into waterways

Mismanaged waste is the source of the plastics we see in rivers, on coastlines and drifting across landscapes far from where they were first used.

Plastic flows on and accumulates.

Rivers act like slow conveyor belts, feeding plastic into the ocean year after year.

Today, an estimated 30 million tonnes of plastic are already in seas and oceans.

Another 100+ million tonnes are trapped in rivers — slowly moving downstream.

Some of this plastic gathers into vast accumulation zones, like the Great Pacific Garbage Patch — a visible reminder of decades of leakage.

At current rates,

plastic waste is expected to triple by 2060.

The crisis grows faster than our ability to handle it.

So… what do we do?

We can't recycle our way out of this problem.

Recycling captures only a fraction of the plastic we create—and most still ends up burned, buried, or lost.

The real opportunity is elsewhere.

Most plastic waste comes from single-use packaging.

Used once, discarded forever.

But reuse systems offer a way forward.

They reduce waste at the source.

They cut emissions.

And they work — when we support them.

The REUSE Foundation exists to accelerate this shift.
